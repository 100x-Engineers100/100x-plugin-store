## Definition
One dominant element per composition. Supporting elements do not compete with the lead. Hierarchy is achieved through size, weight, color, and spacing — not decoration.

## Why It Matters (for design)
Compositions without clear hierarchy make users work to understand what matters. Every extra second of cognitive load is a failure of design.

## How to Apply It
- Identify the single most important thing on the page/screen
- Make it dominant (larger, heavier, more contrast, more space around it)
- Everything else supports it — reduce size, weight, or color intensity of competing elements
- If two things feel equally important, one of them needs to be cut or de-prioritized

## When to Apply It (trigger)
Every layout, every screen, every composition. Ask: "What is the lead element?" If you can't answer in 2 seconds, the hierarchy is broken.
