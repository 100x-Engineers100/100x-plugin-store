## Definition
Standard structure for all SOPs: Purpose → Scope → Owners/Roles → Trigger → Steps → Verification → Escalation → Failure points → Update log.

## Why It Matters (for operations)
SOPs without consistent structure can't be queried reliably and don't transfer ownership cleanly. The framework makes every SOP scannable and executable by someone who didn't write it.

## How to Apply It
Required fields in every SOP:
- **Trigger**: what event starts this process?
- **Owner**: who is accountable (not just responsible)?
- **Steps**: numbered, action-verb-first
- **Verification**: how do you know it's done correctly?
- **Escalation**: what breaks and who gets paged?

## When to Apply It (trigger)
Any new process being documented for the first time.
