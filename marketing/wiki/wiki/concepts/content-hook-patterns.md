## Definition
5 hook types that stop scrolling and earn reading. Derived from audience pain points, not from generic copywriting formulas.

## Why It Matters (for marketing)
90% of content is ignored. The first sentence determines whether the second gets read.

## How to Apply It
| Hook type | Pattern | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Counterintuitive | "[Common belief] is wrong" | "More content doesn't mean more growth" |
| Specific result | "[Person] did [X] in [Y time]" | "We cut campaign build time from 3 days to 3 hours" |
| Pain point | "You're doing [X] and it's costing you [Y]" | "Your copy is honest. That's why it's not converting." |
| Curiosity gap | "Here's what [thing] doesn't tell you" | "What engagement metrics don't tell you about your audience" |
| Direct claim | "[Bold statement]. Here's why." | "Most campaign briefs are useless. Here's why." |

Always derive from ICP pain points in [[icp-targeting]].
