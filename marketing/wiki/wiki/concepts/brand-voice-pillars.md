## Definition
Four pillars that define how all written content sounds: Honest, Sharp, Warm, Direct. Each has a "not" counterpart to prevent misapplication.

## Why It Matters (for marketing)
Without shared voice pillars, content varies by writer and platform. Pillars make consistency achievable without prescribing exact words.

## How to Apply It
- **Honest**: Say what you mean. No jargon. Acknowledge limitations. Honest ≠ brutal.
- **Sharp**: Respect reader's time. Short sentences when both work. Sharp ≠ terse.
- **Warm**: Notice the human. No corporate non-apologies. Warm ≠ sycophantic.
- **Direct**: Lead with the point. Put important info first. Direct ≠ blunt.

## When to Apply It (trigger)
Every piece of written content — copy, email, social, internal comms.
