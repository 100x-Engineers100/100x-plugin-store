# Campaign Planning Framework

> Version 1.0 | Last updated: 2026-05 | Owner: Marketing Lead

---

## Overview

Every campaign we run should answer three questions before a single word of copy is written:
1. **Who exactly are we talking to?** (ICP)
2. **What do they believe right now, and what do we need them to believe?** (Belief shift)
3. **What one action do we want them to take?** (Single CTA)

If you can't answer all three in a sentence each, the campaign isn't ready to brief.

---

## 1. Campaign Brief Template

### Campaign Name
[Short descriptive name — e.g., "Q3 Onboarding Re-engagement" or "Product Hunt Launch"]

### Goal
One sentence. What does success look like? Include a measurable outcome.
*Example: "Drive 500 trial signups from mid-market SaaS companies in India by June 30."*

### ICP for This Campaign
**Primary audience:**
- Role/title:
- Company size:
- Industry:
- Key pain point this campaign addresses:
- Where they spend attention (channels):

**Psychographic profile:**
- What do they believe right now (the wrong belief we're addressing)?
- What does "winning" look like to them?
- What do they fear?

### Belief Shift
*From:* "I believe ___"
*To:* "I now believe ___"

*Example:*
*From: "AI tools are for engineers, not marketers"*
*To: "Marketers who use AI are the ones setting campaign benchmarks in their category"*

### Single CTA
One action. Not two. Not "read our blog and sign up for our newsletter."
*Example: "Start free trial" / "Book a demo" / "Download the guide"*

## 2. AIDA Framework Applied

Every campaign maps to AIDA — but the stages rarely happen in sequence within a single campaign. Define which stage(s) this campaign targets:

### Awareness
**Goal:** They know we exist. They associate us with a specific problem/solution.
**Formats:** Top-of-funnel content, social, PR, SEO
**Metric:** Reach, impressions, new visitors, brand search volume
**Success signal:** Someone says "I've heard of them" before we pitch

### Interest
**Goal:** They want to know more. The problem we solve is relevant to them right now.
**Formats:** Blog, thought leadership, comparison content, case studies
**Metric:** Time on page, content downloads, email open rates
**Success signal:** They're reading our stuff without being asked to

### Desire
**Goal:** They want the specific thing we sell. We're the preferred solution.
**Formats:** Case studies, demos, trial experience, testimonials
**Metric:** Demo requests, trial signups, proposal requests
**Success signal:** They're asking us questions, not competitors

### Action
**Goal:** They buy/sign up/commit.
**Formats:** Sales conversations, onboarding flow, limited-time offers
**Metric:** Conversion rate, revenue, deals closed
**Success signal:** Credit card / contract

## 3. ICP Targeting Matrix

Use this to select channels and formats based on audience profile.

| ICP Characteristic | Recommended Channels | Content Formats |
|-------------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Technical (engineer/PM) | LinkedIn, HN, newsletters, docs | Long-form, technical depth, benchmarks |
| Creative (designer/marketer) | Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, Substack | Visual content, case studies, before/after |
| Executive / Decision maker | LinkedIn, email, events | Data, ROI cases, peer references |
| SMB owner / Founder | Twitter, community, word-of-mouth | Stories, community presence, trust signals |
| Enterprise buyer | LinkedIn, direct outreach, events | Case studies, security/compliance docs |

## 4. Campaign Stages & Timeline

### Pre-Launch (2-3 weeks before)
- [ ] Brief approved by marketing lead and stakeholders
- [ ] Assets in production (copy, creative, landing page)
- [ ] Tracking set up (UTM parameters, conversion events)
- [ ] Email segments defined and tested
- [ ] Channels scheduled and confirmed

### Launch Week
- [ ] All assets live by Day 1 AM
- [ ] Monitor conversion events hourly for first 48h
- [ ] Social posts scheduled across all channels
- [ ] Outreach triggered (email/LinkedIn sequences if applicable)
- [ ] Team notified with campaign brief and context

### Mid-Campaign (after first 7 days)
- [ ] Pull metrics vs. targets
- [ ] Identify worst-performing asset — pause or replace
- [ ] Identify best-performing asset — amplify spend or distribution
- [ ] Update projections for remaining period

### Post-Campaign
- [ ] Final metrics report vs. goals
- [ ] Key learnings (what we'd do differently)
- [ ] Assets archived in campaign folder
- [ ] Wiki updated with campaign post-mortem

## 5. Messaging Architecture

For every campaign, define your messaging at 3 levels:

### Level 1 — Core Claim (10 words or fewer)
The single most important thing we want them to know.
*Example: "Cut your campaign build time from 3 days to 3 hours."*

### Level 2 — Supporting Points (3 maximum)
What makes the core claim believable?
1. [Proof point 1]
2. [Proof point 2]
3. [Proof point 3]

### Level 3 — Evidence
What do we have that proves the supporting points?
- Customer quotes / testimonials
- Data / metrics
- Case studies
- Product demos

Copywriters work from L3 up to L1. Approval happens from L1 down.

## 7. Metrics Reference

| Campaign Type | Primary Metric | Secondary | Health Check |
|---------------|----------------|-----------|--------------|
| Awareness | Reach / Impressions | New users, brand searches | Bounce rate |
| Nurture/Interest | Open rate, CTR | Time on page, downloads | Unsubscribes |
| Conversion | Signups / Demos | CAC, conversion rate | Drop-off point |
| Retention/Expansion | Engagement, NPS | Upsell rate, churn rate | Support tickets |

### Benchmarks (internal)
*Update quarterly in wiki.*

| Channel | CTR target | Conversion target |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Email (campaigns) | > 2.5% | > 5% of clicks |
| LinkedIn (organic) | > 1.5% | — |
| LinkedIn (paid) | > 0.8% | > 3% |
| Landing page | — | > 8% |

## 8. Post-Mortem Template

Fill in within 1 week of campaign close.

```
Campaign: [name]
Duration: [dates]
Goal: [what we set out to do]
Result: [what actually happened — numbers]
What worked: [specific tactics that beat benchmark]
What didn't: [specific tactics that underperformed + hypothesis why]
If we ran it again: [top 3 changes]
Assets worth reusing: [link to best performers]
```
