## Definition
Technique for removing "I'm not technical enough" fear. Reframes the cohort from "coding school" to "understanding AI well enough to direct it, build on it, and sell it."

## Why It Matters (for sales)
"Not technical" is the most common blocker for non-engineers. Without the reframe, the cohort sounds like it's not for them.

## How to Use It
Analogy: "A product manager doesn't write code, but understands it enough to lead engineers. That's what we're building — your ability to direct AI, not write it."

Then go domain-specific (see [[domain-mirroring]] examples).

## When to Use It (trigger)
Lead says "I'm not technical" / "I can't code" / "Is this for engineers?"
